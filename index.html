üîç Individual Stocks vs ETFs: The Complete Breakdown

What Are Individual Stocks?

When you buy an individual stock, you're purchasing ownership in ONE specific company. Example: Buying Apple stock means you only own Apple. If that company fails, you could lose everything.

What Are ETFs (Exchange Traded Funds)?

An ETF is a basket containing hundreds or thousands of individual stocks bundled together. Example: VTI contains over 3,000 U.S. companies. You buy ONE share and own ALL companies inside.

Feature Individual Stocks ETFs
Diversification Zero - all eggs in one basket ‚úì High - hundreds to thousands of companies
Risk Very high - one company can go to zero ‚úì Lower - spread across many companies
Research Required Extensive - study financials, management, industry ‚úì Minimal - automatically diversified
Time Commitment Hours per week monitoring ‚úì Minutes per month
Potential Returns Could be huge OR lose everything ‚úì Matches market average (7-10% annually)
Cost Low trading fees but high risk cost ‚úì Small annual fee (0.03-0.20%)
Recommended For Experienced investors with time & expertise ‚úì Most investors, especially beginners
Why "Just Keep Buying" Recommends ETFs Over Individual Stocks:
1. Individual Stock Picking Usually LOSES

80-90% of professional fund managers fail to beat index ETFs over 10+ years. If pros can't beat the market, individual investors have even worse odds.

2. Concentration Risk is Dangerous

Even "safe" companies can collapse: Enron, Lehman Brothers, GE fell 90%. ETFs automatically drop failing companies and add new winners.

3. Time and Expertise

Stock picking requires 10-20 hours/week of research. ETFs let you focus on your career‚Äîearning more money matters more.

4. Emotional Discipline

When your stock drops 50%, panic selling is common. ETFs are less volatile, easier to hold through downturns.
